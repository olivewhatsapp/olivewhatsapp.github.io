TCG Rules

Individuals and factions struggle for power in the Nexus system, driven by different ideologies. Whoever is the first to establish control over the entirety of Nexus will determine the course of history.

The Factions

Kindness

"Help others to help yourself."
To the green faction, a successful Nexus is one where Cores can be distributed fairly and evenly.
Mechanics: Healing Vestiges, moving Cores between Vestiges, benefiting Supports

Freedom

"Everyone has the right to choose."
To the red faction, a successful Nexus is one where Cores can be freely hoarded or distributed.
Mechanics: Discarding Cores for benefit, high-damage attacks with drawbacks

Security

"Peace cannot exist without order."
To the blue faction, a successful Nexus is one where Cores are tightly controlled to prevent misuse.
Mechanics: Disrupting opposing Vestiges, defensive skills

Balance

"Salvation is the status quo."
To the purple faction, a successful Nexus is one where no one ideology decides how Cores are used.
Mechanics: Recycling used Cores, attacks that care about number of Cores attached

Autonomy

Those who lack a permanent affiliation, serving whatever cause suits them at a given time.
Mechanics: No specific Core type requirements to attack

The Watchers

What are they?


No single worldview is inherently good or evil, and each faction's noble ideologies are as frequently upheld as they are twisted out of shape.
